The Ways I Will Embrace Te Whare Tapa Wha,

& How to Stop Living Like a Portly Nocturnal Bat:

Te Whare Tapa Whā is a model of the 4 dimensions of wellbeing developed by Tā Mason Durie in 1984 to provide a Māori perspective on health, and encompasses four different dimensions; taha tinana (physical wellbeing), taha hinengaro (mental wellbeing), taha wairua (spiritual wellbeing), and taha whānau (family wellbeing) - These four walls have connections to the whenua (land, sea, natrual world) and all form Te Whare Tapa Wha.

Taha Tinana

The first point of call will be physical wellbeing, which I will improve by commiting to leaving the house for walks during foundations, and walking to campus during bootcamp.

I will also focus on getting enough sleep each night, so I am prepared to meet the next morning.

Taha Hinengaro

The second step will be mental and emotional wellbeing, which I will improve by spending less time on my phone in the immediately before and after sleep.

I will also practice a morning chant to get myself ready for the tasks ahead.

Taha Wairua

Third on the list is spiritual wellbeing, whixh I will improve by meditating during the day to refocus and realign my mind.

I will also try to fill in more journal entries to express myself.

Taha Whānau

Fourth is family wellbeing, which I will improve by spending more time with my parents and sisters.

I will also connect more with my friends where possible.

Whenua

Fifth and lucky last is whenua, which will be the easiest to achieve. I will satiate my need for marvelous views by seeking out a scenic once a week, and spending some time enjoying the surrounding landscape (or seascape).

I will also look to hug a tree when I'm feeling extra in touch with Papatūānuku, and look towards the stars when I'm in touch with Ranginui.
