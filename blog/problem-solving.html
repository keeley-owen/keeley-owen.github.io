When I was blocked on a simple problem.

What was the problem?

This example covers when I was faced with an insurmountable challenge, something that would be akin to a trial of Heracles, I was to write a blog post about problems and problem solving. I stared at the screen for minutes that felt like hours. A simple task, yet I felt the burden of extreme memory fog on my shoulders like a pack full of concrete...

What problem solving techniques did you use?

I did some mental pseudocode, and thought each step out logically and in plain english, there were many times I had been stumped by a simple problem (it happens daily, and frequently at that) so I used my massive bonce to think of what I was currently facing - then the answers fell upon me like rain in July.

How did you feel throughout the process?

Initially I was thinking of tasks that had been simple yet stressed me out, but I was struggling to articulate the problem and how I felt because I couldn't remember the details, or had decided something else was more appropriate. This left me feeling a bit stressed, so when I realised I am currently facing a problem that is simple - But I was overcomplicating it was natrual to switch and nail down this instead, I feel good typing this.

What did you learn?

Less stress, take a deep breath and chill for a minute - Instead of worrying I decided to write something that was easy to think about, and work through it bit by bit.

Elegantly solved a problem.

What was the problem?

I was unhappy with how my blog was presented, I felt it was lacking in charm and was unappealing (needed more flair).

What problem solving techniques did you use?

Thought out what the issues with my blog were, and if it was purely aesthetics or if I disliked the content as well. Decided it was a bit of both and that some pictures might liven it up.

How did you feel throughout the process?

Decided to add more images from Open at the Met (open source) that fit with the theme of each page while I was writing my blog, this helped me keep engaged with what I wanted it to look like and add some flavour.

What did you learn?

Adding pictures made it look more appealing, and adding funny alts made it crack up for myself.
