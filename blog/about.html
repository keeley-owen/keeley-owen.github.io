A bit of info about myself

Values, Strengths, and Limitations.

I've grown up with the value of kindness being a main attribute, which to me means helping where help is needed and trying to see things from different points of view - For instance, if someone likes coriander it would be impolite to berate them for this choice no matter how egregious it may seem. Growing up in the idyllic Paekakariki has let me to an appreciation of ocean views (despite a lacklustre swimming capability) which has since broadened to enjoy scenics of all shapes and sizes ; whether it be a mountain range, a grassy plain, or even our great Poneke city scape - The harbour here being a particular place I enjoy. I think my culture has played a huge part in shaping the man I am today, and while I may not be as in touch with my whakakapapa as I would like to be, I understand that doesn't lessen my cultural connection and I can still rep my set with pride - Something that is literally coursing through my blood. In terms of people who shaped me, my friends have helped created a rigorous training regiment of witty humour and aligned interests from differing spectrums of media, literature, and music. I'm pretty happy with the progress I have made to become the person I am now, and am confident I will continue to grow into my self with time - Aging like a fine cask of Whiskey.

My strengths are mainly not technical, however I believe in my capability to learn as I've always had a thirst for knowledge, I love learning about new things as gaining new skills feels gratifying - I think I have good interpersonal skills which will help me in this course, as being able to relate to people from all ages and backgrounds is something that I am able to do through years of training (having customer centric roles throughout my working life) and hard work (needing to have a job to survive). I think a willingness to learn will assist me the most during this course, as even when it may take me awhile to grasp some concepts I will give it my best attempt and not let setbacks get me down, as the gratification of completing something outweighs the hair-tearing issues that are likely to come up.

My limitations are few and far between, being a being carved out of granite with the wisdom of Solomon and the dashing good looks of , however if I were to look at it with a non delusional frame of mind I would say that I learn best by doing, and sometimes visual and reading aids simply do not stick until I have attempted something (which sometimes may take several attempts). I understand my limitations involved with joining this course, I have not done any coding in the past and have limited exposure to programming languages, taking into account that is the main focus of this course I need to be prepared to put in extra work where required and remember not to compare myself to my compatriots. I think having everything we go over be something 'new' may end up being overwhelming at times, so I will need to make sure to avoid burn-out and information fatigue .

Ethics and working a team.

An ethical decision I have made is choosing to work for companies that have values that align with my own, I've worked for big businesses that are mainly centred around their profits, with their impact and values coming secondary to this main focus. While toiling away as another cog in the machine I learned that a lot of diversity and impact reduction was mainly done as a way to save face and not with these beliefs as intrinsic values to adhere to. I felt like being a part of these and perpetuating the cycle was not good for me and what I believe in, so I committed to only working for places where their values matched my own, I don't want to be a part of a business that doesn't help do their part in minimising their impact since this is something I have complete control over.

An example on where I have been trying to work productively was was in a previous job role of mine, the task was to perform remediation on a part of the business that had been under review and as a result there were a number of clients due refunds of a substantial amount. This task involved coordination with multiple departments with people of varying skillsets and positions within the company, this melting pot of people unfortunately resulted in a disjointed and wonky implementation - The way I managed this was by offering to take on additional tasks and trying to explain where the roadblocks were occurring oft fell on deaf ears until things went wrong, after which feedback was sought from a variety of staff. This taught me that even if you think you have the answers, certain things are out of your control so the best practice is to not let things outside of your control affect you and to focus on the things you can change.
