Learning plan.

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

I'm unsure at this point, I know what I don't want to do but with the possibilities of something I *do* want to do being so broad, I'm not certain on what pathway will be the right fit - I do know that I want to work somewhere that values me and is genuine about this, I also want to do something that makes me feel proud. I definitely know what type of 'feeling' I want for my career even if I don't know where that will be.

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

My strengths lay in drive and application, once I've got the general gist of something I find it easy to branch out and try new things, and do not fear exploring topics or tasks deeper. A limitation would be sometimes I don't realise I have a block, and will try to complete a task instead of trying something else for a while (often to my own detriment) which is something I'm actively trying to work on. I also find it difficult to ask for help from other people, but I have no problem doing independent research to see if I can find the answers myself.

What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I would like to see development of my communication and team work skills, I think this course will help greatly in this regard due to the structure of working as a team in the cohorts as well as group/paired assignments.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme. This should include:

An explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, Facilitators and industry/community representatives What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

I will commit myself to working productively by not overloading myself too often, I have a tendency to take on too many things at once and end up stuck, often feeling like it's too overwhelming to do anything - I have been working on this by focusing on a small amount of things throughout the day, breaking it down in bits rather than stressing over the scope of everything. In terms of working with others, I will actively try to contribute where possible and make sure other's suggestions and feedback is taken on board, by making sure I'm actively being a team player and contributing it will help those I'm working with feel like their effort is being validated. I will being good chat and vibes where possible.

Seeking help in a timely way will be something I need to work on, I research things a lot and make sure I understand them conceptually before getting stuck in, so being more comfortable to ask my facilitator and cohort questions will be something that will come as I get more comfortable with the course.

Support when things get hard, sometimes it will not necessarily be I'm stuck on technical tasks, but might need somebody to talk the problem through and bounce ideas off so I can come to the solution. So far knowing there is someone to go to if things take a turn makes a big difference as I know if I need help, I can reach out, which removes the anxiety of things that seem super complex.
